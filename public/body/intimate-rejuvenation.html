About Intimate Rejuvenation

As life takes its course, a woman’s body keeps changing. Childbirth, hormonal changes, weight fluctuations and the passage of time, all have an impact. Various factors – genetics included – influence how the body responds to these events and the extent to which it recovers. These changes take place all over, and the intimate areas are no exception. Vaginal tissue may get overstretched, collagen may be damaged and the muscle tone of the vaginal wall, weakened.

As a result, quality of life may be affected in various ways. At a physiological level, these changes also lead to conditions such as stress urinary incontinence, vaginal atrophy, dryness and recurring infections. Physical intimacy could get compromised, which can go on to erode self confidence and place relationships under undue stress. Intimate areas deserve special attention. That’s why at Lmar, our dermatologists have applied their expertise to bring together a very special set of solutions that restore and rejuvenate the intimate area with effective results, working to the highest safety standards.

Our Intimate Rejuvenation Treatment Include

  • Intimate Rejuvenation
  • Intimate tightening and firming
  • Intimate contouring and reshaping

As with any other part of the body, the intimate areas are a complex system of skin and muscle. As time goes by, skin may darken. Muscles lose firmness and turn lax. Collagen depletes leading to a loss of volume, shape and contour. Lmar Intimate Rejuvenation is a blend of cosmetic dermatological procedures that gently, yet firmly turn back the clock on all of these changes. It restores vulvovaginal aesthetics and wellbeing at any age or stage in life – most commonly after childbirth and the peri or postmenopausal phases.

While childbirth can cause vaginal laxity, the loss of collagen is a more common cause. As time goes by, tissue loses thickness. Not only does this affect appearance, it also reduces the ability of the inner mucosal membranes to do their job, turning intimacy into an uncomfortable experience. Our dermatologists generally use specialised fillers to restore thickness and revive youthful firmness. Vaginal diameter is reduced, and the area turns tighter and more responsive to sensory stimuli.

Ageing affects the intimate areas and the fresh, youthful look tends to wear away. In order to address vaginal atrophy, as the condition is called, treatment must restore lost volume and remodel the area. Our dermatologists may recommend one of or a combination of carboxy therapy and custom blended filler treatments for the purpose.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have a question not listed below? Send us your Question to find more answers and info about our complete range of laser, skin, and cosmetic procedures.

  • WHAT CAN I EXPECT DURING THE PROCEDURE?

    Topical anesthetic cream is applied and local anesthetic may be injected to numb the treatment areas. PRP (platelet rich plasma) is injected in the anterior vaginal wall from underneath the bladder neck to the G spot and also to the glands of clitoris. Minimal discomfort is expected as treatment areas are numbed prior to commencement of treatment.

  • HOW MANY TREATMENTS ARE REQUIRED?

    Usually once yearly, but this could differ depending on the patient’s individual symptoms.

  • WHAT CAN I EXPECT AFTER THE PROCEDURE?

    You may return to your normal activities immediately. Return to normal sexual activities is also immediate. You may feel hypersensitive for the first few days after the procedure and also have an increase in sensation. This will subside over the next two weeks.

  • WHO ARE GOOD CANDIDATES FOR THIS PROCEDURE?

    Post-partum women
    Post-menopausal women
    Women that suffer from recurrent vaginal infections
    Women with mild to moderate stress urinary incontinence
    Women with Vaginal dryness
    Women who feel they have vaginal laxity or looseness
    Women who experience pain during intercourse
    Women with low sex drive

  • WHO ARE NOT GOOD CANDIDATES FOR THIS PROCEDURE?

    Women who have moderate to severe vaginal, uterine, or a prolapsed bladder
    Women with pacemakers or implantable defibrillators
