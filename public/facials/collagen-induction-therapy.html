About Collagen Induction Therapy

Automated Micro-Needling (also known as Collagen Induction Therapy or CIT) is a new innovation in aesthetic medicine for the treatment of fine lines, crepey skin, acne scars and improvement of the skin’s overall appearance.

During this procedure, tiny needles are used to create controlled micro-injuries to the skin in order to produce collagen and elastin. The skin’s repair process results in a thicker dermis with the appearance of softer wrinkles. The Dermal micropen also creates superficial micro-channels which allow topical gels, creams and serums to be absorbed more effectively into the skin.

Your skin will appear to be visibly healed after 24-48 hours but the healing process is continuously happening beneath the surface for several months. This natural healing process is what makes the treatment so effective.

Prior to treatment:

Avoid Active Skin Infections or Conditions: If you have active skin infections, such as cold sores or active acne outbreaks, it's advisable to wait until these conditions have healed before undergoing Collagen Induction Therapy. The treatment can potentially worsen or spread the infection.

Immediately after treatment:

Your skin will appear bright red and inflamed. We suggest you do not apply make-up. We also recommend you combine your microneedling or nano needling with an Intraceuticals facial to aid healing and give the active ingredients more opportunity to absorb.

Patients notice an immediate “glow” to their skin. Visible changes to the skin develop over the course of several days and weeks. Results continue to improve up to 6 months after the treatment as collagen production continues.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have a question not listed below? Send us your Question to find more answers and info about our complete range of laser, skin, and cosmetic procedures.

  • How is the procedure done?

    A sterile needle-tip containing 12 tiny micro-needles is attached to the Dermal MicroPen. Your skincare professional will apply a thin layer of highly potent hyaluronic acid (super hydrating serum) and vitamin C to your skin and select the appropriate treatment depth based on your unique needs. Next, in a single motion, the pen will be gently pressed against the skin while simultaneously gliding in one direction until the entire treatment area has been covered. This results in thousands of microscopic channels throughout the entire treatment area.

  • What does it Feel Like?

    The feeling associated with the Dermal Micropen is similar to light sandpaper being moved across the skin. While some areas are more sensitive than others, the speed of the reciprocating needle tip reduces discomfort while the applied hydrating gel also makes the treatment more tolerable.

  • How long does the procedure take?

    The procedure takes 15-30 minutes depending on the size of the area treated.

  • How many treatments will I need?

    Some patients only require a single treatment, once per year to achieve optimal results. However, it is recommended for most patients to receive a series of 2-4 treatments spaced about 4-8 weeks apart. For patients with deep wrinkles, advanced photo-aging, stretch marks or acne scars it is recommended to receive 6-8 sessions at 4 week intervals.

  • When is skin needling treatment not suitable?

    the treatment should not be performed within 2 weeks of having an injectable.

    the treatment is never performed on raised moles, warts or lesions

    during pregnancy (when breastfeeding the treatment is allowed)

    medication (some medication may reduce healing time, please advise your therapist on anything you may be taking)

    the treatment will not be performed if the client has an active herpes or acne breakout, however, once resolved the treatment can go ahead after 2 weeks
