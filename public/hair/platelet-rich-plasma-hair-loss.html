About Platelet-Rich Plasma for Hair

Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) is a medical treatment that uses the patientâ€™s own blood plasma to address hair loss. PRP treatment involves four steps.


1- Blood is drawn and put in a centrifuge machine to separate the different phases according to their densities.

2- The centrifuge process is complete blood will be separated into three phases: platelet-rich plasma, platelet-poor plasma, and red blood cells.

3- Only the platelet-rich plasma is extracted from the other two phases with the help of a syringe.

4- Extracted platelet-rich plasma is injected into the scalp to promote hair growth.


Each injection takes approximately 15 seconds. PRP treatment for hair loss is a quick and easy process that provides ultimate patient comfort.

Consultation: Schedule a consultation at LMAR Clinic to discuss your hair loss concerns. Our experts will evaluate your condition and determine if PRP is a suitable treatment option for you.

Medical History: During the consultation, provide details about your medical history, including any underlying conditions or medications you are currently taking. This information helps us customize the treatment plan to suit your specific needs.

Scalp Examination: Our healthcare professionals will conduct a thorough examination of your scalp to assess the extent of hair loss and determine the appropriate treatment approach.

Follow Instructions: Adhere to the aftercare instructions provided by our healthcare professionals, which may include using specific hair care products or medications to enhance the healing process.

Patience: Hair regrowth takes time, and the results of PRP treatments may not be immediate. It's important to be patient and continue with the treatment plan as advised by our experts.

Combination Therapy: In some cases, combining PRP with other hair loss treatments, such as topical medications or low-level laser therapy, may enhance the overall effectiveness. Our healthcare professionals can discuss these options with you based on your specific situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How does PRP treatment work?

    PRP can be a good choice to stop hair loss and strengthen thinning hair thanks to its rich content of growth factors, proteins, and thrombocytes. Various studies show that PRP promotes natural hair growth and increases hair thickness by increasing the blood flow to the hair follicle. Since PRP is rich in proteins and vitamins, it is also considered to be a suitable solution for hair loss caused by nutritional deficiencies.

  • Who is a good candidate for PRP hair treatment?

    People who are suffering from thinned hair and moderate hair loss are good candidates for this treatment. Also, people who are looking to enhance the results of a hair transplant can consider a PRP hair treatment for improved hair follicle health. People who are intimidated by the idea of hair transplant surgery can choose to get a PRP hair treatment to stop or slow down hair loss. The use of blood thinners, heavy smoking, and certain disorders might be an obstacle for PRP hair treatment. You can contact our medical consultants for a detailed consultation.

  • Is PRP hair treatment safe?

    As PRP hair treatment involves the use of patientâ€™s own blood plasma, it is considered safe when performed by a trained professional. Occasionally, side effects such as mild pain, redness, headaches, and temporary hair shedding may be observed after PRP hair treatment.

  • Is PRP hair treatment painful?

    The discomfort during the PRP hair treatment procedure is minimal. When the blood is drawn slight ordinary discomfort might be involved. Local anaesthesia is usually applied to minimise the discomfort of the injection process.

  • Is PRP treatment better than a hair transplant?

    Hair transplant is another treatment used to solve hair loss. This process involves removing healthy hair follicles from usually back of the head and transplanting these hair follicles to the target area to increase the hair density and restore the healthy hair look of patients.

    PRP treatment focuses on strengthening the existing hair follicles and promotes natural hair growth by using natural platelets in the patientâ€™s blood. Hence, these treatments work on completely different principles. Whether a hair transplant or PRP treatment will be the best option for you depends on the specific condition of your hair.
